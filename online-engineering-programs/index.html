Accreditation

Accreditation is a key consideration for students who are considering pursuing an online engineering degree. Accreditation is a process by which an educational institution or program is evaluated by an external body to ensure that it meets certain quality standards. Accreditation is important because it assures students that the program they are enrolled in is rigorous and credible, and that their degree will be recognized by employers and other educational institutions.

There are a number of different accrediting agencies for engineering programs, including the Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology (ABET). ABET is a non-profit organization that accredits engineering programs at colleges and universities throughout the United States. ABET accreditation is the most widely recognized accreditation for engineering programs in the United States, and it is considered a mark of quality for engineering education.

When choosing an online engineering program, it is important to make sure that the program is accredited by a reputable accrediting agency. Accreditation ensures that the program meets certain quality standards, and that your degree will be recognized by employers and other educational institutions.
