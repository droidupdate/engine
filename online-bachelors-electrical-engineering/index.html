Accreditation

Accreditation is an important consideration for students who are considering an online bachelor's in electrical engineering program. Accreditation ensures that a program meets certain quality standards and that its graduates are prepared to enter the workforce. Online programs that are accredited by the same accrediting bodies as traditional on-campus programs have undergone a rigorous review process and have met the same high standards.

  • ABET accreditation: The Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology (ABET) is the most widely recognized accrediting body for engineering programs in the United States. ABET-accredited programs meet the highest standards of quality and ensure that graduates are prepared to enter the workforce.
  • EAC accreditation: The Engineering Accreditation Commission of ABET (EAC) is the specialized accrediting body for electrical engineering programs in the United States.
  • Other regional accrediting bodies: In addition to ABET, there are also several regional accrediting bodies that accredit online bachelor's in electrical engineering programs. These bodies include the Middle States Commission on Higher Education (MSCHE), the New England Commission of Higher Education (NECHE), and the Western Association of Schools and Colleges (WASC).

When choosing an online bachelor's in electrical engineering program, it is important to make sure that the program is accredited by a reputable accrediting body. Accreditation ensures that the program meets high quality standards and that graduates are prepared to enter the workforce.
