  • Licensing

    In Georgia, engineers are licensed by the Georgia Board of Professional Engineers and Land Surveyors (GA BPLS). To obtain a license, engineers must pass the Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) exam and the Principles and Practice of Engineering (PE) exam. The FE exam is typically taken after completing a bachelor's degree in engineering, and the PE exam is typically taken after working in the field for several years.

  • Accreditation

    The Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology (ABET) is the accrediting body for engineering programs in the United States. ABET accreditation ensures that an engineering program meets certain quality standards, including having a qualified faculty, adequate resources, and a rigorous curriculum.
